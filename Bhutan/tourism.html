Sights

  • Tiger's Nest Monastery (Paro Taktsang): This iconic monastery is perched on a cliff at an altitude of 3,120 meters (10,240 feet) above sea level. It is one of the most famous and sacred sites in Bhutan, offering breathtaking views of the Paro Valley.
  • Punakha Dzong: Built at the confluence of the Pho Chhu and Mo Chhu rivers, Punakha Dzong is a majestic fortress and former capital of Bhutan. It is renowned for its beautiful architecture and is one of the most photogenic dzongs in the country.
  • Rinpung Dzong (Paro Dzong): This fortress-monastery is located in the Paro Valley and is known for its intricate woodwork and stunning frescoes. It also hosts the Paro Tsechu, one of Bhutan's most important religious festivals.
  • National Memorial Chorten (Thimphu): Located in the capital city of Thimphu, this stupa is a prominent religious monument and a center for daily worship. It is dedicated to the memory of the third king of Bhutan and is a popular pilgrimage site.
  • Bhutanese Traditional Arts School (Thimphu): Also known as the Institute for Zorig Chusum, this school preserves and promotes traditional Bhutanese arts and crafts. Visitors can witness students learning skills like painting, sculpture, and weaving.
  • Dochula Pass: Situated on the way to Punakha from Thimphu, Dochula Pass offers panoramic views of the eastern Himalayas. The pass is adorned with 108 chortens and is a peaceful place to take in the surrounding beauty.
  • Phobjikha Valley: Known for its stunning natural beauty, this glacial valley is a protected area and home to the endangered black-necked cranes during their winter migration. It is also the location of the Gangtey Monastery.
  • Haa Valley: A picturesque valley that has only recently been opened to tourists, Haa Valley offers a glimpse into traditional Bhutanese rural life. The valley is surrounded by hills and is dotted with ancient temples and monasteries.
  • National Folk Heritage Museum (Thimphu): This museum provides insight into Bhutanese rural life, showcasing traditional artifacts, tools, and exhibits on the country's rich cultural heritage.
  • Jigme Dorji National Park: For nature enthusiasts, this national park offers diverse flora and fauna, including takins (Bhutan's national animal), red pandas, and various bird species. The park covers a vast area, providing opportunities for trekking and wildlife exploration.
