General Facts


Capital: Thimphu
King: Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck
Currencies: Bhutanese Ngultrum, Indian Rupee
Official language: Dzongkha
Population: 777 486 (2021)
Calling code: +975
Bhutanese Cuisine: Ema Datshi (chili and cheese stew), momo (dumplings), and red rice.
Religion: Buddhism


Landscape

  1. Himalayan Mountains: The northern part of Bhutan is dominated by the Eastern Himalayas. Some of the world's highest peaks, including Gangkhar Puensum, the highest unclimbed mountain, are located in Bhutan.

  2. Valleys: Central Bhutan is characterized by deep valleys, where most of the country's population resides. These valleys are often covered with terraced fields, and the landscape is dotted with traditional Bhutanese architecture.

  3. Lush Forests: Bhutan is known for its extensive and pristine forests, covering a significant portion of the country. The government places a strong emphasis on environmental conservation, and the lush forests are home to diverse flora and fauna.

  4. Glacial Lakes: The mountainous regions of Bhutan are home to several glacial lakes. The country is mindful of the potential threats posed by glacial lake outburst floods (GLOFs) and actively monitors and manages these lakes.
